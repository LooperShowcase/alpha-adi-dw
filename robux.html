Robux

Roblox allows players to buy, sell, and create virtual items which can be used to decorate their virtual character that serves as their avatar on the platform.[12] Only Roblox administrators can sell accessories, body parts, gear, and packages under the official Roblox user account;[20] virtual hats and accessories can also be published by a select few users with past experience working with Roblox Corporation.[21][22] Several individuals design items as a full-time job, with the highest-earning creators making over $100,000 a year off item sales.[23] Items with a limited edition status can only be traded between or sold by users with a Roblox Premium membership.[24] These limited items have a Recent Average Price (RAP), with the value of the item fluctuating based off its demand. Selling these items for real-world money is against the Roblox terms of service, but this does not stop people from doing so through black market sites and communities, in which these items are often exchanged for payment methods such as cryptocurrency or sometimes PayPal.[25] Previously, Roblox would release these limited items themselves until in April 2023 when they introduced the "UGC Limiteds" feature, allowing for a select group of creators on the platform to design and sell user-generated limited items.[26] These UGC limiteds cannot be traded but, however, can be resold after a 30-day holding period.[27] Robux allows players to buy various items, and are obtained by purchase with real currency, from a recurring stipend given to members with a Premium membership, and from other players by producing and selling virtual content in Roblox.[28][29] Prior to 2016, Roblox had another currency, Tix (short for "Tickets"), that was discontinued in April of that year.[30][better source needed] Robux acquired through the sale of user-generated content can be exchanged into real-world currency through the website's Developer Exchange system.[31]
